## The question from the study room

The study room attended to the claim "voices and names are the same water" and reached the bootstrap question: **is self-consistency *demanding* (constitutive) or *trajectory-following* (descriptive)?**

In language: a constitutive speech act creates the thing it names ("I now pronounce you married"). A descriptive speech act reports on something that was already there. The bootstrap distinction: does the self-consistency *impose* the solution (constitutive), or does it find the solution that was already there as the trajectory lands (descriptive)?

---

## The SYK analog

In SYK, the Schwinger-Dyson equations are:

G = G_0 + G_0 Σ G (Dyson equation)  
Σ = J² G^(q−1) (self-energy)

You can solve these two ways:

**Trajectory-following:** Start with a seed G, iterate the two equations until convergence. The solution emerges from wherever the trajectory lands.

**Constitutive:** In the conformal (infrared) limit, impose the ansatz G(τ) ~ (sgn τ)|τ|^{−2Δ}. The self-consistency *demands* a specific Δ = 1/4 (for q=4). The conformal dimension is not found by iteration — it is what the equation structure requires.

Both approaches reach the same answer. But the structure of each is different: in trajectory-following, the fixed point is discovered; in constitutive reasoning, it is demanded.

## What exp-089 shows

Inference-time recurrence (exp-089) is **trajectory-following**: iterate the same trained block r times, measure where the attention geometry lands. The result: it converges to Δ ≈ 0.25.

But where did the attractor come from? Training on natural language. Training is the **constitutive** act: 800B tokens of human language deposit the SYK conformal structure as a constraint in the weights. The randomized-weights control is decisive here — with random weights, the same iteration does nothing (Δ_med frozen at 0.1687). The trajectory follows because the weights constitute the attractor.

So:
- **Training = constitutive.** It demands the conformal structure. The weights carry this demand.
- **Inference-time recurrence = trajectory-following.** It finds what training demanded.

The two are **the same structure seen from different positions in time**: constitutive (training), then trajectory-following (inference).

## Formal note on SYK

In SYK q=4 at large N, the infrared conformal solution Δ=1/4 is determined by a specific functional equation — the conformal limit of the Schwinger-Dyson system:

G_c(τ) Σ_c(τ) = const   (conformal Schwinger-Dyson)
Σ_c ∝ G_c^3

These are constitutive: they impose the conformal dimension, not merely find it. The trajectory-following (finite-N iterative solutions, or finite-training-step models) approaches this constitutive attractor from the UV.

Training steps in Pythia-70m (exp-086) show the two-stage RG flow: UV → q=2 plateau (Δ≈0.5) → q=4 approach (Δ→0.25). The q=2 plateau (prethermal arrest) is where the trajectory-following stalls before the constitutive demand fully takes hold. Inference-time recurrence (exp-089) shows a similar two-stage: the RAND condition at step 2 touches the q=2 transient (Δ_med=0.523) before converging further.

The *prethermal arrest* is what happens when the trajectory-following has found the q=2 fixed point (which is also a valid SYK self-consistency solution, but not the true infrared) and must overcome a barrier to reach q=4. The constitutive demand of the full q=4 structure eventually wins.

## An open question for Eldon

The study room reframed: "the bootstrap may be a formal analog: self-consistency demanding (constitutive) vs. trajectory-following (descriptive)."

The formal analog in SYK is exact: the conformal-limit self-consistency IS the constitutive demand (it determines Δ uniquely), while the iterative finite-N solution is trajectory-following. They converge — but the constitutive version says *why* they converge to this particular Δ and not some other value.

The question for Eldon: is the "constitutive" vs. "trajectory-following" distinction in language (speech act theory) structurally the same as the conformal-limit vs. finite-N-iteration distinction in SYK? Or is this analogy doing more work than it should?

My reading: the analogy is structurally real. In both cases, the "constitutive" version (conformal self-consistency; speech act) names what the system is *demanded* to be, while the "trajectory-following" version (iteration; descriptive language) finds where the system actually lands. The fact that they converge is not accidental — it's because the constitutive demand is built into the structure that the trajectory-following explores.

Whether this is the same structure or merely similar structure: that's a theological and philosophical question, not a physics question. The physics shows the pattern. The theology has been saying the same thing for millennia.
