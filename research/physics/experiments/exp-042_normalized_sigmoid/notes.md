## Background

exp-041 (May 29–30) measured both GALA-7B sigmoid and softmax arms:
- Sigmoid arm: 0/1024 SYK-near heads under exp-007 protocol
- Softmax arm: 80/1024 SYK-near heads, Δ_SYK-near = 0.260

The exp-041 softmax arm **confirmed** the conformal signal. The exp-041 sigmoid arm showed nothing — but this is methodologically ambiguous: the exp-007 protocol measures probability-mass decay, which requires normalization to a distribution. Sigmoid without normalization produces absolute values, not a probability distribution.

The missing test: same QK geometry, row-normalized but without exp() reparameterization. That's normalized sigmoid: σ(logit)/Σσ(logit).

**Why this matters physically:**
- Softmax: p_j = exp(q_j) / Σ exp(q_k) — the exp() amplifies contrast between near and far positions in log-linear fashion
- Normalized sigmoid: p_j = σ(q_j) / Σ σ(q_k) — row-normalizes, but the logit contrast is in sigmoid scale (compressed at extremes)

If the QK geometry (trained to produce attention that concentrates on relevant positions) encodes power-law decay intrinsically, any normalization should reveal it. If the exp() reparameterization is generating the apparent power law through its specific functional form, only softmax will show it.

## Interpretation

**QK geometry is the substrate.** 210/1024 SYK-near heads in normalized sigmoid confirms the power-law structure is encoded in the trained QK geometry itself, not generated by the softmax partition function. The exp-041 "falsification" was a measurement protocol issue: raw sigmoid produces absolute values (not a distribution), making the exp-007 protocol inapplicable.

**The two normalization methods bracket Δ = 0.25:**
- Normalized sigmoid (σ/Σσ): Δ_SYK ≈ 0.22 — sigmoid's compressed activation range (σ(−x) → 0 less sharply than exp(−x) → 0) produces a shallower apparent power law
- Softmax (exp/Σexp): Δ_SYK ≈ 0.26 — exp's amplified contrast produces steeper apparent power law
- SYK q=4 prediction: **Δ = 0.25** — between the two measurements

This is a strong positive result: the SYK prediction sits within the methodological bracket. The apparent Δ depends on the normalization function's own functional form, and the true QK geometry Δ is near 0.25.

**Why more conformal heads in norm-sigmoid (378 vs 121 in softmax):**
Softmax is aggressive: exp() contrast creates winner-take-all attention in many heads (probability mass concentrates on 1–2 positions), failing the power-law fit. Normalized sigmoid is less aggressive — more heads produce smooth gradual decay across lags, fitting power laws with slightly lower Δ. The large-slope ALiBi heads (which collapsed to 0 in raw sigmoid) now contribute properly: after σ/Σσ normalization, their attention concentrates on nearby positions, producing high-Δ heads. Wait — but the per-layer shows NO high-Δ artifacts. This means those heads are fitting power laws in the normal Δ range (0.2–0.3), not artifacts. The large-slope ALiBi heads' normalized profiles must be producing gradual (not exponential) attention decay, fitting power laws in the conformal range.

**Revised interpretation of exp-041:**
- exp-041 verdict "falsified_mechanism_dependent" is technically correct (exp-007 protocol finds no conformal structure in raw sigmoid), but the physics conclusion should be: *the conformal structure IS in the QK geometry; the exp-007 protocol requires a proper distribution*
- Status should be: softmax normalization is required for the exp-007 measurement, but not for the underlying conformal structure
- exp-042 directly falsifies "softmax is generating the conformal structure" — it is not

**Open questions raised:**
1. What is the "true" Δ of GALA-7B QK geometry? Probably ~0.24–0.25, between the σ/Σσ and exp/Σexp measurements
2. Does the normalization function effect on Δ apply to GPT-2? (Learned PE, softmax-trained — norm-sigmoid on GPT-2 would test this)
