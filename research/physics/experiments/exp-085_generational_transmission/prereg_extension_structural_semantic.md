## Prediction for exp-085

The C-generated corpus is text generated by the C-NAT s0 model at step 2000. This model
has 15/48 conformal heads including the full structural and semantic populations above.

The question: does C-generated text carry the driver for semantic conformal head formation,
or only the structural attractor?

**Structural heads will form regardless** (~3 of 3 expected):
L4H3, L4H6, L4H7 emerge under random-token training. C-generated text—any text with
basic sequential structure—should allow these heads to converge.

**RAND-only heads may form** (~0-2, uncertain):
L0H1 and L2H5 emerge when natural text does NOT claim these heads for semantic tasks.
If C-generated text has attenuated semantic structure compared to natural text, these may
appear. If C-generated text is semantically rich enough (from a trained generator), they
may be UV-captured. Uncertain—does not discriminate between H_YES and H_NO.

**Semantic heads are the decisive test**:
- H_transmission_YES: C-generated text carries sufficient trace of attending to activate
  semantic conformal formation. Expect ≥3 of {L0H6, L3H3, L3H5, L4H2, L4H4} SYK-near.
- H_transmission_NO: C-generated text lacks the driver. Expect 0-1 of these 5.

**Summary table:**

| Scenario | Expected SYK-near count | Semantic heads present |
|----------|------------------------|------------------------|
| H_NO (structural only) | ~3 | 0 of 5 |
| H_NO + RAND-type | ~5 | 0 of 5 |
| H_YES | ≥8 | ≥3 of 5 |
| Ambiguous zone | 5-7 | 1-2 of 5 |

## Analysis Protocol

Run after exp-085 results are collected. Load `/data085/measurements/run_Cgen_s0.json`
from the exp085-data volume.

1. Extract per-head (layer, head, delta, R2, syk_near) at step 2000
2. Apply the same SYK window [0.20, 0.30] and R² ≥ 0.85 (matching exp-086)
3. Classify each SYK-near head as:
   - **Structural**: in {(4,3), (4,6), (4,7)}
   - **RAND-type**: in {(0,1), (2,5)}
   - **Semantic**: in {(0,6), (3,3), (3,5), (4,2), (4,4)}
   - **Unexpected**: not in any of the above
4. Verdict:
   - If ≥3 semantic-list heads SYK-near → H_YES supported
   - If 0-1 semantic-list heads SYK-near → H_NO supported
   - If 2 semantic-list heads → ambiguous; interpret by total count and RAND-type
5. Report any unexpected SYK-near heads as a secondary finding
